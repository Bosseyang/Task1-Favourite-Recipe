Recipe: Bolognese

This recipe is my own take of the famous Italian Bolognese. The reason to why this is my favourite recipe is because it is a very filling recipe with alot of depth in taste and works equally well as a leftover dish as when eaten fresh. I usually do not limit my use of Bolognese with just pasta, I make Bolognese sandwiches, pizza etc with it aswell. It also servs perfectly as a meat sauce base for lasagna!

Ingredients

This recipe is for four (4) servings

  • 500g Ground Beef
  • 2 Shallots
  • 2 Garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1 chicken bouillon cubes
  • 2dl Red Wine, using water or milk is fine
  • 1 tbsp Honey
  • 2 tbsp Tomato Puree
  • 1 Can of Crushed tomatos
  • 2dl Heavy Cream
  • 1tsp Dried Oregano
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 1 tbsp Light Soy Sauce
  • Pepper
  • Salt
  • Optional: 3 Grated Carrots (Optional)
  • Chilli flakes (Optional)

Instructions

  1. Peel the shallots and the garlic, chop the shallots and finely chop the garlic. Grate the carrots if you choose to use it, it gives a little sweet taste and blends in perfectly, whilst making the Bolognese a little juicier.
  2. Sauté the shallots and garlic along with the grated carrots in some olive oil a couple of minutes.
  3. Add the tomato puree and sauté for another minute.
  4. Add the ground beef and stir fry until cooked evenly.
  5. Crumple and add the bouillon cube, mix in the crushed tomatoes, wine/water/milk, heavy cream, bay leaf, oregano, honey, soy sauce and optionally the chilli flakes. Makes sure to stir so everything blends together.
  6. Let simmer with a lid on for about 30 minutes, the longer the better. season with salt and pepper after your own taste.
